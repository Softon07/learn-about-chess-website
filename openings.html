Best Chess Openings

Spanish Game

A classic opening in which white starts with the move 1.e4 e5, followed by 2.Sf3 Sc6.

Italian game

An opening in which white starts with the move 1.e4 e5, followed by 2.Sf3 Sc6 and 3.Bc4. The Italian game is characterized by dynamic development of the figures and the struggle for the central fields.

Scotch Game

An opening in which white starts with the move 1.e4 e5, followed by 2.Sf3 Sc6 and 3.d4. The Scottish game leads to a dynamic battle for the center and often leads to sharper positions.

Queen's gambit

An opening in which white offers a pawn on f7 (e.g. 1.e4 e5 2.f4) in exchange for initiative and dynamic play. The king's gambit is often used as a way to surprise the opponent and create sharp positions.
